Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 28, 2025
Last Updated: March 28, 2025

CleanJSON ("we," "our," or "us") operates the website located at cleanjson-web.vercel.app (the "Site"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you visit and use our Site.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 JSON Data You Input

CleanJSON is a client-side application. All JSON data you paste, type, or drag into the tool is processed entirely within your web browser using JavaScript. Your JSON data is never transmitted to our servers or any third-party service. We do not collect, store, log, or have access to the content of your JSON input at any time.

1.2 Usage Analytics

We may collect anonymous, aggregated usage data to understand how the tool is used and to improve the user experience. This may include:

  • The length (character count) of JSON input — not the content itself
  • Whether the JSON was valid or invalid
  • The type of error encountered (if any)
  • Browser type and version (user agent string)
  • Timestamp of the action

This data is collected via a fire-and-forget webhook and contains no personally identifiable information (PII). The collection is optional and can be disabled by the site operator.

1.3 Local Storage

CleanJSON uses your browser's localStorage to store:

  • Theme preference — whether you prefer light or dark mode.
  • Visitor count — an anonymous count of visits for display purposes.

This data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to any server. You can clear this data at any time through your browser's settings.

2. Third-Party Services

2.1 Google AdSense

We may use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our Site. Google AdSense uses cookies and web beacons to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and other websites. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to our Site and/or other sites on the internet. You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings. For more information about how Google uses data, please visit Google's Privacy & Terms.

2.2 Adsterra

We use Adsterra to display advertisements on our Site. Adsterra may use cookies and similar technologies to collect information about your browsing activities to provide targeted advertisements. For more information, please refer to Adsterra's Privacy Policy.

2.3 Google Analytics

We may use Google Analytics to collect anonymous usage statistics such as page views, session duration, and geographic region. Google Analytics uses cookies to identify unique visitors. No personally identifiable information is collected. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

2.4 Vercel (Hosting)

Our Site is hosted on Vercel. Vercel may collect standard server logs including IP addresses, browser type, and access timestamps for security and performance purposes. For more information, visit Vercel's Privacy Policy.

3. Cookies

CleanJSON itself does not set any cookies. However, third-party services integrated into our Site (Google AdSense, Adsterra, Google Analytics) may set cookies on your device. These cookies are used for:

  • Serving and personalizing advertisements
  • Measuring ad performance
  • Collecting anonymous usage statistics

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of advertisements but will not affect the core JSON formatting features of CleanJSON.

4. Data Retention

Since we do not collect or store your JSON data, there is nothing to retain or delete. Local storage data (theme preference, visitor count) remains on your device until you clear your browser data. Any anonymous usage analytics data collected via our webhook is retained indefinitely in aggregate form and cannot be linked back to individual users.

5. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

  • Right to Access — you may request information about what data we hold about you (note: we hold no personal data).
  • Right to Deletion — you may clear all locally stored data via your browser settings.
  • Right to Opt Out — you may opt out of personalized advertising via the links provided in Section 2.
  • Right to Object — you may object to data processing by contacting us.

6. Children's Privacy

CleanJSON is a general-purpose developer tool and is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete such information.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this page periodically for the latest information about our privacy practices.

8. Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
